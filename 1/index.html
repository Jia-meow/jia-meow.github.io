Retinal circuitry modeling

Early detection of movement plays a key role in threat awareness and appropriate registration of optical flow guides self-locomotion and posture maintenance. Direction selective pathways underlying motion detection have been well studied in mouse and rabbit due to easy accessibility. At retinal level, ON-OFF direction selective ganglion cells (ooDSGC) convey direction selective signals by eliciting robust spiking of action potentials to preferred direction and eliciting minor/no response to null direction. Surprisingly, no evidence shown direction selectivity (DS) in DSGCs from primate retina until recently. Dr. Daceyâ€™s lab, which we will collaborate with, proposed that DS presents in recursive bistratified retinal ganglion cell in macaque monkey for the first time. Animals with forward-facing eyes have different optic flow fields from animals with sideways-facing eyes, implying different direction selective mechanisms. Starburst amacrine cell (SAC) is considered as the origin of DS in retina, which connects post-synaptically to bipolar cells, presynaptically to DSGCs and connects pre- or post-synaptically to neighboring SACs. Direction selective calcium signals were recorded from the dendritic branches of SAC in primate retina. However, the mechanisms of its direction selectivity remain unclear. Our central hypothesis is that the intrinsic cell morphology and electrophysiology, the array of bipolar cell inputs and the reciprocal inhibition from neighboring starburst amacrine cells together comprise the direction selectivity of starburst amacrine cell in primate retina. The aim is to build a computational SAC model (in Neuron C simulator) with pre-synaptic connections and biophysics using realistic cell morphology to investigate the dendritic directional tuning of SAC.
